# Collaboration Manifesto & Contributor Guidelines

Welcome to the **EU Health Education One Health Repository**. Developed within the framework of the **Beacon COST Action (CA24106)**, this project aims to map the integration of One Health (OH) and Planetary Health (PH) across EU-27 school curricula. 

By joining this consortium, you are contributing to a high-impact, Open Science infrastructure designed for publication in leading peer-reviewed journals, including ***The Lancet Planetary Health***, ***WIREs Climate Change***, and ***Scientific Data***.

---

## 1. Our Values: Transparency & Rigor
We operate under the framework of **Critical Realism**. Our methodology strictly separates the **Empirical Domain** (raw, official legislative and curricular texts) from our **Analytical Domain** (AI-assisted thematic extraction and synthesis). 

Your role as a **Country Expert** is to bridge these domains through human validation, ensuring that the unique "Real" mechanisms of your national education system are accurately captured and interpreted.

---

## 2. What’s in it for You? (Authorship & Credit)
To ensure fair and transparent recognition of all contributions, we follow the **CRediT (Contributor Roles Taxonomy)**. This model satisfies the reporting requirements of major European grants and the Beacon COST Action.

* **Co-authorship (Data Descriptor):** All active contributors who validate their country’s data will be invited as co-authors on the **Data Descriptor paper** (Target: *Scientific Data*).
* **Thematic Collaboration (Main Analysis):** Deep contributors providing qualitative regional insights or theoretical synthesis will be considered for co-authorship in our **Main Analysis papers** (Targets: *The Lancet Planetary Health* for health-centric outcomes; *WIREs Climate Change* for interdisciplinary climate education reviews).
* **Global Visibility:** Your name and affiliation will be featured in our interactive **Streamlit Dashboard** and the repository’s `CITATION.cff` file, generating formal citations for your role as a data curator.
* **Direct Data Access:** You gain immediate, full access to the harmonized EU-wide dataset for your independent regional or comparative research within the Beacon network.

---

## 3. Your Workflow (The "Low-Friction" Path)
We value your time. Our AI-driven pipeline performs the "heavy lifting," allowing you to focus on high-level validation:

1.  **Level I Validation (`master.yaml`):** Verify the national laws, decrees, and curriculum documents listed for your country. Ensure they are the latest versions (as of 2026).
2.  **Level II Validation (`analysis.jsonl`):** Review the thematic snippets extracted by the AI. Verify translation accuracy and confirm whether the **Explicite/Implicite** labels reflect your national context.
3.  **Qualitative Insight (`review_[ISO].md`):** Provide brief commentary on "Generative Mechanisms"—how do your country's specific environmental or health challenges (e.g., heatwaves, zoonoses, forest management) drive these educational priorities?

---

## 4. Technical Standards
* **Language:** All metadata, summaries, and official communications are conducted in **English** to ensure interoperability.
* **License:** This project is licensed under **CC BY 4.0**. You retain full credit for your contribution while allowing the broader scientific community to reuse and build upon the data.
* **Tools:** We use **GitHub** for version control and **Streamlit** for visualization. No coding expertise is required; you can edit documents directly via the GitHub web interface.

---

## 5. How to Join
1.  **Contact the Lead Architect:** Reach out to **[Paweł Krzyworzeka/pkrzyworzeka@kozminski.edu.pl]** to be added as an official collaborator.
2.  **Access the Repository:** Once added, create a branch for your country (e.g., `feature/poland-update`).
3.  **Validate & Submit:** Complete the validation files in your country's folder and submit a **Pull Request**.
